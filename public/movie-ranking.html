The Best Movies According to Johan

My top 3 movies of all-time.

I am Legend

I Am Legend" is a 2007 post-apocalyptic science fiction thriller film starring Will Smith. Set in a near-future New York City, the story follows Dr. Robert Neville, a brilliant scientist who is seemingly the last human survivor after a man-made plague has wiped out most of humanity. The virus has turned the majority of the population into nocturnal mutants called Darkseekers, who are highly aggressive and sensitive to UV light.

Neville, immune to the virus, spends his days roaming the deserted city, scavenging for supplies, and attempting to find a cure using his own blood. He lives with his loyal dog, Sam, who provides companionship in the otherwise desolate environment. At night, Neville barricades himself in his fortified home to stay safe from the Darkseekers.

As the film progresses, Neville discovers signs of other survivors and must navigate the psychological toll of isolation, the constant threat of the Darkseekers, and his hope to save humanity. The movie is a gripping exploration of survival, the human spirit, and the consequences of scientific experimentation gone awry.

The film's haunting visuals of an empty New York City and Smith's powerful performance make "I Am Legend" a memorable and thought-provoking cinematic experience.


The Hobbit (3 movie series)

"The Hobbit" is a fantasy adventure film series based on J.R.R. Tolkien's classic novel, directed by Peter Jackson. The series consists of three movies: "An Unexpected Journey" (2012), "The Desolation of Smaug" (2013), and "The Battle of the Five Armies" (2014).

The story follows Bilbo Baggins, a peaceful hobbit from the Shire, who is reluctantly drawn into an epic quest by the wizard Gandalf. Bilbo joins a group of thirteen dwarves led by the brave Thorin Oakenshield, whose goal is to reclaim their homeland, the Lonely Mountain, from the clutches of the fearsome dragon Smaug.

The films are rich in themes of bravery, friendship, and the journey of self-discovery. The sweeping landscapes of Middle-earth, stunning visual effects, and epic battles are hallmarks of Jackson’s adaptation. Each film delves into the lore and history of Tolkien’s world, providing a deep and immersive experience.

"The Hobbit" series serves as a prequel to the "Lord of the Rings" trilogy, blending adventure, fantasy, and heartwarming moments. It's a tale of how a small hobbit becomes a crucial player in the grander saga of Middle-earth, showcasing the importance of courage and the impact of unexpected heroes.


The Book of Eli

"The Book of Eli" is a 2010 post-apocalyptic action film directed by the Hughes Brothers and starring Denzel Washington. Set in a desolate, dystopian future, the movie unfolds in a world ravaged by an unspecified cataclysmic event that has turned the earth into a barren wasteland.

The story centers on Eli, a solitary wanderer, played by Denzel Washington, who has been journeying across the United States for thirty years. He is driven by a singular mission to deliver a sacred book, the last known Bible, to a safe location in the West. The book holds the key to humanity's survival and renewal, and Eli is deeply committed to his task, guided by a strong sense of faith and purpose.

The film explores themes of faith, belief, and the power of hope in a world devoid of morality and order. Eli’s unwavering commitment to his mission contrasts with the selfish ambitions of others around him.

The desolate, sun-bleached landscapes, crumbling ruins, and scarce resources highlight the harshness of this future world. The cinematography captures the stark beauty and danger of the setting.

"The Book of Eli" combines intense action sequences with a contemplative narrative about spirituality and the human condition. Eli’s journey is marked by brutal encounters with marauders and moral dilemmas.

"The Book of Eli" is a gripping and thought-provoking film that melds the intensity of a survival story with profound reflections on faith and the power of knowledge. Denzel Washington's compelling performance, along with the film's striking visuals and thematic depth, makes it a memorable exploration of the lengths one will go to protect and preserve what they believe in.
